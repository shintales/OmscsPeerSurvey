CS6460-SP24
PrisMXR: A Curated Art Museum Experience in VR with Audience Input Functionalities
PrisMXR is an art and archaeology VR experience application intended for investigating the value of audience input, which is traditionally an indispensable part of curatorial activities in art education, on the learning outcome of art and archaeology education through virtual gallery experiences. Audience input, in line with what we observe in conventional art galleries or museums, includes commenting, critiquing, additional annotations, and notes on guestbooks. These activities cultivate the dialog between the curator and the public and enhance the collaboration among visitors, both of which are highly meaningful in the learning motivation, outcome, and problem-based learning for art and archaeology

CS6460-SP24
AttentiveMind – Study tool to improve focus and learn efficiently
AttentiveMind is a development track project and study tool positioned to help students with attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) or those experiencing short attention spans learn, study, and retain information more efficiently. The tool includes features such as textual summarization based on inputs of lecture transcripts, note-taking assistance including highlighting, sharpening/blurring, and color coding, flashcard generation, and key concept extraction. The goal is to create a holistic study tool that students can use alongside watching or reading lectures, to help them take notes efficiently and build a better understanding of the material. The following questions will ask you to review images of the interface and provide feedback on your impressions of the tool.

Digital Distractions in Online Learning Environments
This research explores the impact of digital distractions on learners of various age groups in online learning environments. This research aims to obtain data across different age groups on the susceptibility of digital distractions which can be used to devise methods to tackle digital distractions. Your privacy and data protection are important to us. All information collected in this survey will be kept confidential and used solely for research purposes.
